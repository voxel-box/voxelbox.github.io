Yours to run. Ours to protect.

When VoxelBox builds something for you, you get a license to use it for your own project — for as long as you like. The underlying code, design, and assets we create stay VoxelBox property. They may not be sold, modified, reverse-engineered, or redistributed without our written permission. This page explains exactly where the lines are.

What you're free to do.

Everything we deliver comes with a broad license to use it in your own business. You never have to ask permission to simply run and rely on what we built.

Operate the delivered work for your own brand or business — publicly and commercially, with your own customers using it every day.

No resale or transfer

You can't sell, resell, sublicense, rent, or hand the work — or any part of it — to a third party.

03 — who owns what

Clear lines on ownership.

A license to use is not a transfer of ownership. Here's how it splits by default.

Stays VoxelBox's

All original source code, design systems, components, and creative assets we author — plus the reusable frameworks, tooling, methods, and know-how we develop before and during your project. We reuse those across our work; they are never sold off with a single build.

Stays yours

Logos, brand assets, copy, files, and data you provide remain 100% yours. You give us permission to use them to deliver your project — we never claim ownership of your material.

Transfers only in writing

Nothing beyond your license moves to you unless a signed VoxelBox agreement says so. If it isn't written down and agreed, VoxelBox retains its rights.
