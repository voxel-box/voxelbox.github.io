A living AI town.

A persistent virtual town where AI residents live out their days — chatting, forming opinions, and remembering you between visits.

the problem

What we were handed.

“We use AI” is easy to say and hard to show. Screenshots of chatbots all look the same, and a demo that resets every session proves nothing about running agents for real.

We wanted a piece of proof you can walk around in — AI characters with memories, relationships, and plans, running continuously on our own hardware rather than rented API calls.

what we built

The system.

Voxelburg: a persistent virtual town where AI residents live out their days — chatting with each other, forming opinions, and remembering visitors between sessions.

The agent simulation, the language models, and the world state all run on VoxelBox's self-hosted AI stack. It isn't a scripted demo; it's the same agentic machinery we use for business automation, wearing a friendlier face.

Residents

Agents with memories, relationships, and daily plans of their own.

World

A persistent simulation that keeps running whether or not anyone's watching.

Models

Self-hosted LLMs doing the thinking — no per-conversation API bill.

Front door

Walkable in the browser, by anyone, any time.

the outcome

What changed.

A public, always-on demonstration of agentic AI infrastructure — the same building blocks we use for client work, running where anyone can poke at them.
