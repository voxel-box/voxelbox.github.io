From first lead to launch day.

the problem

What we were handed.

Winning a client is only step one. Leads, proposals, build status, and launch checklists were scattered across chats, notes, and memory — and the gap between “they said yes” and “the site is live” is exactly where projects stall.

Client Radar solved the top of the funnel, but a spreadsheet was still playing project manager for everything after.

what we built

The system.

Launch OS: a lead-to-launch platform that carries every prospect through one pipeline — from the first automated lead sweep, through proposal and build, to a launch checklist that gates go-live.

It grew out of Client Radar and absorbed it as the front of the funnel, so a business found by the sweep and a project shipping next week live in the same system, at different stages.

Pipeline

Lead → proposal → build → launch, as explicit stages with owners.

Radar intake

Client Radar feeds the funnel automatically.

Checklists

Launch gates so nothing ships half-finished.

One screen

The status of every project, without asking anyone.

the outcome

What changed.

One screen answers “where is every project right now.” Nothing lives in someone's head, and nothing falls through the crack between sales and delivery.
